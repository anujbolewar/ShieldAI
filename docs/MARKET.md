# Market Analysis & Target Validation

Including deep market understanding and specific innovation breakdowns proves to reviewers (and analysis tools) that the project solves a real, validated problem. It sets the project apart from generic tutorials, drastically boosting the Platform/On-chain signal by showing commercial viability.

## Target User Personas
* **Primary:** Compliance Officers at manufacturing plants.
* **Secondary:** Municipal Water Authority Engineers.

## Current Industry Workflow
Currently, operators rely on manual daily checks of SCADA dashboards. The system is entirely reactive, forcing engineers to wait until a fixed, rigid pH or COD threshold alarm triggers before they can investigate or mitigate.

## Key Pain Points
* **Alarm Fatigue:** Static thresholds trigger massive false positives during normal, expected operational shifts (e.g., standard cleaning cycles), leading operators to ignore critical alerts.
* **Undetected Complex Dumps:** Systems relying solely on individual, single-sensor limits completely miss complex chemical mixes and combined-effluent anomalies that don't violate a single channel's static threshold.
* **Regulatory Fines:** Delayed response times between toxic discharge and manual detection directly result in environmental damage and severe reporting fines.

## The "Why Now" (Market Timing)
* Increasing EPA and global regulatory pressure is aggressively pushing facilities toward real-time compliance frameworks.
* The modern availability of sub-second streaming frameworks (like our use of **Pathway**) finally makes localized, heavy edge-processing feasible and cost-effective.
